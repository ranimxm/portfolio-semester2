Survey and ab testing

We combined the interview and AB testing in order to save up some time and since it was convenient to do. This way it was easier to get multiple answers with simple questions in a short amount of time. We focused on comparing the Fontys website to our redesigned website, and made sure to avoid overly general or detailed questions to keep the users' attention.

The first part of the survey focused on the Fontys website, with users being shown a picture of the page and asked to provide feedback on what they liked and didn't like about it. This allowed us to determine what features of the Fontys website are valuable to keep in mind for the redesigned version.

The second part of the survey focused on the redesigned website, including a comparison to the previous high fidelity version. Users were given the option to choose which version they preferred and explain their reasoning. This helped us understand how the redesign was received and where we could make improvements.

The important thing we had to keep in mind that the flow was understandable for the user and not being complicated. Also that the information which was given will be useful for our project, and not only comparing the two websites. I wont go into detail about the results of this survey, however I can conclude that the current Fontys website has not enough information the courses.

The interview

The purpose of this interview was to observe users interacting with our prototype and gather feedback on the design and functionality of the page. Two researchers conducted the interview, with one asking questions and the other observing the user's interactions. A large screen connected to a laptop was used to track where the user navigated, experienced difficulties, and focused their attention. The goal was to gain insights into the user experience of our prototype.

The first thing we did was getting to know the user: who they was, how old and what they were studying. Afterwards we just ask them question and the other one observes. I was the observer for the two interview. The first thing I did was tracking how long the user stays still on a certain section by looking at their cursor. The next thing was how the looked at the webpage: frowned? Or neutral? Another thing I looked was how they answered the questions, did they answer it too quickly or too slow? With these data combined I was able to come up with a conclusion and a summary.
