The Gestalt Principles

Research about the principles

    There are six gestalt principles in the UX:

  1. Figure/Ground: We tend to perceive elements as either the foreground (figure) or the background (ground). This helps us distinguish objects from the environment around them;
  2. Similarity: Elements that are similar to each other are perceived as a group. This is because users tends to group things together that share similar characteristics, such as color, shape, or size;
  3. Proximity: Elements which are close to each other will be perceived as a group. This is because the humanmind tends to group things together that are close in a certain amount of space;
  4. Continuety: The eye tends to follow lines or curves, which can cause elements to be perceived as a group. For example, if you see a series of dots arranged in a curved line, your eye will tend to follow the line and perceive the dots as a group, rather than as individual elements;
  5. Closure: When elements are arranged in a way that suggests a complete form, even if it is not actually complete. We tends to fill in the missing pieces and perceive a complete whole. For example, if you see a circle with a small gap in it, your mind will tend to fill in the gap and perceive the circle as complete;
  6. symmetry and order: The humanmind tends to perceive elements as organized and symmetrical. This is because symmetry and order are often associated with stability and predictability, which are important for our survival.

With these in mind I had to use the group's fidelity and recreate it using the gestalt principles.

The recreation

The left side is the fideltiy of our group and the right side is with the changes I made.

The first change was to the section with the specializations. By changing the menu from a vertical orientation to a horizontal one, I utilized the gestalt princple of contiuation. This principle states that the eye tends to follow lines or curves, which helps guide the users attention and creates a more visuallized experience. I also modified the arrows to point dirrections, to draw the user's attention to the important elements.

The second change was to the section with the examples. I used the principle of similarity to arrange the grey boxes and text symmetrically. This princple states that elements that are similar to each other are perceived as a group and arranging elements symmetrically can create a sense of balance and organization.

The third change was to the grey boxes in the examples. By giving the boxes different widths, I applied the principle of proximity. This principle states that elements that are close to each other are perceived as a group, so seperating the grey boxes with different widths helps the user perceive them as individual elements rather than a single group.
